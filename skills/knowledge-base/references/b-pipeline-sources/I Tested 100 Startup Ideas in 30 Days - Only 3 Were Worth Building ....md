I Tested 100 Startup Ideas in 30 Days - Only 3 Were Worth Building (Here's the Exact Framework I Used)

John Daniel M

https://www.indiehackers.com/johndanielm1999

Hey there! I'm John Daniel M from

, and after 5 years of building platforms and watching countless startups crash and burn, I decided to do something crazy.

I was tired of seeing entrepreneurs (including myself) waste months building products nobody wanted. So I challenged myself: Could I validate 100 startup ideas in just 30 days using a systematic approach?

The result? 97 ideas were complete garbage. But those 3 winners? They're now generating over $180K in combined revenue, and two of them are on track to hit 7 figures this year.

Here's the brutal truth: Most people spend more time choosing their Netflix show than validating their startup idea. I'm about to change that for you.

The "97% Failure Rate" Reality Check

Before we dive into the framework, let me share what I discovered during this experiment:

I was optimistic. Every idea seemed brilliant. I thought I'd found 20 winners.

Reality hit. Most ideas crumbled under basic scrutiny.

I became ruthlessly systematic. The framework started working.

Only 3 ideas survived every test I threw at them.

The shocking part? The 3 winners weren't the "sexy" AI or blockchain ideas. They were boring, simple solutions to annoying problems that people actually pay to solve.

The 4-Phase Validation Framework That Changed Everything

Phase 1: The 60-Second Idea Filter

Time per idea: 1 minute

Goal: Eliminate obvious losers

#### I created a rapid-fire checklist:

\[ \] Can I explain this in one sentence?

\[ \] Does this solve a problem I've personally experienced?

\[ \] Would I pay for this solution?

\[ \] Can I identify 10 specific people who have this problem?

100 ideas → 40 ideas (60% eliminated immediately)

#### Real Example:

"AI-powered sock matching app" failed because I couldn't name 10 people who'd pay for it. "Freelancer invoice tracking tool" passed because I knew dozens of freelancers struggling with this.

Phase 2: The Google Test

Time per idea: 5 minutes

Goal: Validate market demand

#### For each surviving idea, I searched:

"\[Problem\] solution" - Are people actively searching?

"\[Problem\] software" - What competition exists?

"\[Problem\] reddit" - Real people discussing this pain?

#### The Magic Numbers:

Search volume: 1,000+ monthly searches

Competition: Less than 5 direct competitors

Reddit discussions: At least 3 threads with 50+ comments

40 ideas → 15 ideas (62% eliminated)

#### Winner Example:

"Local business review management" had 8,100 monthly searches, only 2 major competitors, and tons of frustrated restaurant owners on Reddit.

Phase 3: The 48-Hour Customer Discovery Sprint

Time per idea: 2 days

Goal: Talk to real humans

This is where most people fail. They assume instead of asking. Here's my exact process:

Day 1: Find Your People

LinkedIn: Search job titles related to your target market

Facebook Groups: Join 3-5 groups where your customers hang out

Cold outreach: Send 20 personalized messages

Day 2: The Interview Blitz

Goal: 5 conversations minimum

Script: "I'm researching \[problem area\]. Do you have 10 minutes to share your experience?"

#### Key questions:

How do you currently handle \[problem\]?

What's the most frustrating part?

What would an ideal solution look like?

Would you pay $X/month for that solution?

#### The Brutal Filter:

If I couldn't get 5 people to talk about the problem, the idea died.

15 ideas → 7 ideas (53% eliminated)

Phase 4: The MVP Reality Check

Time per idea: 3-4 days

Goal: Build the absolute minimum to test demand

#### I didn't build apps. I built tests:

#### Landing Page MVP (Day 1-2):

Single page explaining the solution

"Coming Soon" signup form

Pricing information

Social proof (even if it's just testimonials from discovery calls)

#### Traffic Test (Day 3-4):

$50 Google Ads budget per idea

Target keywords from Phase 2

Track: Click-through rate, email signups, pricing page views

#### The Success Metrics:

Landing page conversion: 3%+ email signups

Ad click-through rate: 2%+

At least 10 people willing to pre-order/pre-pay

The Framework Template (Copy This)

Week 1: Idea Generation & Filtering

#### Monday-Tuesday:

Brainstorm 25 ideas per day (use problem-solving frameworks)

Apply 60-second filter to all 50 ideas

#### Thursday-Friday:

Google Test on remaining ideas

Week 2: Customer Discovery

Find and contact potential customers for top 15 ideas

#### Tuesday-Friday:

Conduct interviews (aim for 5 per idea)

Analyze feedback and eliminate weak ideas

Week 3: MVP Creation

#### Monday-Tuesday:

Build landing pages for remaining ideas

#### Wednesday-Thursday:

Set up tracking and analytics

Launch ads and start collecting data

Week 4: Analysis & Decision

#### Monday-Wednesday:

Analyze all metrics

Make final cuts based on data

Create development roadmap for winners

The Uncomfortable Truths I Learned

Your favorite idea will probably fail.

I was emotionally attached to a "revolutionary" project management tool. It got zero interest.

Boring problems pay better.

The sexiest idea (AR shopping app) got lots of "cool!" responses but zero paying customers.

Speed kills perfectionism.

Moving fast forced me to focus on what actually mattered: customer pain.

Most "competition" is actually validation.

If others are solving similar problems, it means there's a real market.

Revenue cures everything.

Once money started flowing, all the "but what about features X, Y, Z?" questions disappeared.

Your 30-Day Challenge Starts Now

I'm challenging you to test 10 ideas (yes, just 10) using this framework over the next 30 days. Why 10? Because you'll actually finish, and finishing beats perfecting.

Generate and filter 10 ideas

Customer discovery on top 5

Build MVPs for top 3

Analyze and pick your winner

No coding until Phase 4

Talk to real humans in Phase 3

Kill ideas ruthlessly based on data, not emotions

Set a timer for each phase

Ready to Stop Guessing and Start Building?

The difference between successful entrepreneurs and everyone else isn't better ideas—it's better validation.

Stop building what you think people want. Start building what they're already trying to buy.

Your 30-day validation journey starts with idea #1. What problem did you face this week that made you think "there has to be a better way"?

That's your starting point.
