# Strategic Discovery Agent: Vibe Graphing Intelligence Layer

A **Strategic Discovery Agent** within the Vibe Graphing methodology functions as a multi-modal **"Intelligence Layer"** that transitions from passive scraping to autonomous signal processing. This agent identifies **market tension** by synthesizing data from diverse, non-Reddit environments to find "painkillers"—solutions to immediate, costly, and recurring operational failures.

## 1. Multi-Platform "Sentry Stack" (The Ingestion Layer)
To discover pain points beyond Reddit, the agent utilizes a diverse array of data streams:
*   **B2B Review Mining (G2 & Capterra):** Filters for **1-star and 2-star reviews** of popular enterprise tools, searching for phrases like "doesn't have," "wish it could," or "too complex for small teams". 
*   **Automation Gaps (Upwork & Fiverr):** Scrapes job boards for repetitive tasks being outsourced (e.g., "manual data entry from PDF to spreadsheet"). *Logic: If 100+ people pay $20/hour for a manual task, they will pay $50/month to automate it.*
*   **B2C Mobile Frustration (App Stores):** Analyzes negative reviews on top-rated apps to identify missing features users are "screaming for" (e.g., offline mode).
*   **Organic Intent (Search Console Regex):** Uses advanced **Regular Expression (Regex)** filters in Google Search Console to identify long-tail, conversational queries (10+ words) troubleshooting complex, unsolved issues.

## 2. Trigger and Pattern Detection (The Analysis Layer)
Calibrated to detect **trigger events** forcing technology stack re-evaluation:
*   **Pricing Shocks:** Sudden cost increases or feature-gating by incumbents (e.g., Notion pricing changes).
*   **Enterprise Drift:** Tools becoming too bloated/costly for original SMB users, creating an **"80/20 Gap"** (need 20% of features, pay for 100% complexity).
*   **Platform Arbitrage:** Spotting features successful on one platform (e.g., Airtable forms) missing on another (e.g., Notion)—identifying a **"temporary tooling vacuum"**.
*   **The "Ugly Glue" Signal:** Patterns where users stitch tools with complex Zapier workarounds (4+ zaps) or "ugly spreadsheets".

## 3. Agentic Synthesis and Scoring (The Decision Engine)
Uses **Gemini Deep Research** or **n8n workflows** for structured intelligence:
*   **Pain Intensity Scoring:** Score (0–100) based on **Recurrence Velocity** (daily vs. annual), **Revenue Proximity** (blocks cash flow?), and **Linguistic Density** (long rants as blueprints).
*   **Competitive Gap Analysis:** Prioritizes niches with **<5 direct competitors** or where incumbents hide pricing behind "Book a Demo" gates.
*   **Technical Feasibility Audit:** Utilizes **MCP (Model Context Protocol) registry** to search ~900 indie tools to prevent "token reinvention".

## 4. Dialogue-Led Validation (The Execution Layer)
Transitions to **"Discovery through Dialogue"** to vet real intent:
*   **Personalized Outreach:** Uses LinkedIn Sales Navigator or Twitter/X DMs to find ICP-aligned individuals.
*   **The "Mom Test" Interrogation:** Instead of pitching, the agent drafts questions focused on **past behavior** (e.g., "Walk me through the last time you faced this [problem]") rather than future intentions.
*   **The Data-Commitment Truth Serum:** To confirm a "painkiller" opportunity, the agent initiates a **Workflow Diagnostic Invite**—asking if the user would commit to a 3-field self-service "audit" to see their exact cost of friction. *If they won't provide their workflow data to solve it, the problem is a "vitamin," not a "painkiller".*
