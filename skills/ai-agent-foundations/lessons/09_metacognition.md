# Lesson 09: Metacognition in AI Agents

## Introduction

Metacognition refers to higher-order cognitive processes that involve thinking about one's own thinking. For AI agents, this means being able to evaluate and adjust their actions based on self-awareness and past experiences.

## Key Concepts

### Why Metacognition Matters
- **Self-Reflection**: Agents can assess their performance and identify improvements
- **Adaptability**: Agents can modify strategies based on feedback
- **Error Correction**: Agents can detect and correct errors autonomously
- **Resource Management**: Agents can optimize time and computational power

### Components of Metacognition

1. **Planning**: Outlining steps to achieve a goal
2. **Evaluation**: Assessing outcomes and performance
3. **Self-Correction**: Adjusting actions based on evaluation

### Corrective RAG

Combines retrieval with self-correction:
- **Prompting Technique**: Specific prompts to guide retrieval
- **Tool**: Algorithms to evaluate relevance
- **Evaluation**: Continuous assessment and adjustment

### Bootstrapping with Goal

Starting with clear objective before iterating:
1. Define clear goal
2. Create initial plan
3. Iterate to refine
4. Optimize for objective
