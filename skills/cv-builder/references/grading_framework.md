# CV Grading Framework (2026 Standards)

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| ATS Compatibility | 25% | Keyword density, formatting, structure |
| Content Quality | 25% | Achievements, metrics, clarity |
| Technical Skills | 20% | Range, relevance, depth |
| Career Trajectory | 15% | Trajectory, growth, consistency |
| Industry Alignment | 15% | Role fit, domain expertise |

## Grade Scale

| Grade | Score | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| A+ | 95-100 | Top 1% - Exceptional |
| A | 90-94 | Top 5% - Outstanding |
| A- | 85-89 | Top 10% - Excellent |
| B+ | 80-84 | Top 20% - Very Strong |
| B | 75-79 | Above Average |
| C | 60-74 | Average |

## Strengths Indicators

### Exceptional (A+)
- 15+ years experience
- Quantified achievements (£M+)
- Multiple industries
- Leadership experience

### Strong (A)
- 10+ years
- Measurable impact
- Technical breadth
- Progression visible

## Common Weaknesses

- No quantified achievements
- Vague responsibilities
- Missing keywords
- Formatting issues
- Too short or too long
