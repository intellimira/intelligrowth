For months, I've been building MIRA (my AI agent system) with a fundamental limitation: every time I ask MIRA to think, that reasoning happens in someone else's cloud.

That's about to change.

OpenJarvis is Stanford's local-first AI framework. Instead of sending my prompts to OpenAI or Anthropic, the entire cognitive stack runs on my machine:
- Ollama for local inference
- OpenJarvis agents for orchestration
- My own models (Qwen3, Llama3.2)
- Zero latency, zero data exposure, zero recurring costs

This isn't just a tech upgrade. It's a paradigm shift:

→ Before: MIRA = Orchestra conductor borrowing instruments
→ After: MIRA = Complete symphony (conductor + instruments + concert hall)

The best part? All my HITL (Human-In-The-Loop) protocols remain intact. More autonomy for MIRA, more trust because nothing leaves my machine.
